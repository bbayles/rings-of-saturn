ToyCo History

 

2034

Shenji Watanabe wins the 39th annual Robot Sumo Contest, beating several factory-sponsored competitors. Honda offers Watanabe a private, no-strings grant to develop his design ideas, but he rejects the offer. Patent process begun for the mobile robotic artificially intelligent device under the name "Rover."

2035

Watanabe sets up a small workshop to produce Rovers with commercial applications --- assistants for disabled people, guard units, emergency drones, and toys. The small shop employs nine people under the name ToyCo.

2037

Formal offers to purchase ToyCo from Hyundai, Honda, and Matsushita are declined.

Watanabe develops the injected alloy-planing machine primarily for use in the toy Rover line, in order to make the small robots more durable and to give them a less boxy, sleeker appearance.

2038

Watanabe’s book, "The Trickiest Problems Know When You’ve Solved Them," is published worldwide and becomes an international best seller.

Fire destroys the ToyCo warehouse manufacturing building. The new, larger manufacturing plant is relocated to Kobe.

Watanabe’s son, Junji, begins work on the production floor of the new plant.

2041

ToyCo sets up its first foreign manufacturing site in San Jose, California.

2042

Junji Watanabe designs "Flower," a mechanical kitten Rover programmed to behave as a loving pet for a child.

2043

Sales of the $400 "Flower" kitten exceed all abilities to supply the Rover. New plant built in Kobe in order to help meet the demand.

2044

Manufacturing facilities open in Denmark and Spain, and the San Jose plant expanded to twice its capacity. "Flower" is still the top seller, but recognition of the ToyCo brand is helping to sell all Rovers.

2045

Ursine companion to "Flower," the teddy bear Rover, "Honey," introduced. As expected, huge market demand develops for the new Rover.

2046

Junji Watanabe named Vice President of Design and Development.

2047

Rover patent approved, though other robot architectures have also been designed and released by engineering competitors.

2050

$750 Million contract signed to develop intelligent automated peacekeeper Rovers for the United Nations Standing Army.

2051

Company restructures to form three functionally separate divisions underneath the ToyCo umbrella --- one to handle security, emergency, and defense Rovers, one to build high-end commercial Rovers, and one that produces the relatively inexpensive toy robotics and derivative toy products.

2053

ToyCo takes lead role in a partnership with Nissan Automotive Manufacturing Company to create a line of adaptively intelligent public and private transportation Rovers. The mission behind this effort is to resolve the congestion of the central computer-controlled Japanese roadway system by giving individual vehicles more autonomy from the traffic server.

Marketing campaigns also target free-roadway nations, especially Canada, the U.S., and Russia.

2054

ToyCo accepts invitation to be a full member of the United Nations Global Economic Council.

2056

Fourth main division of the company created. This division is charged with the design, production, and sales of non-military passenger vehicle Rovers.

2057

ToyCo votes against Brute Force membership on the GEC.

2062

Shenji Watanabe retires. His son, Junji, named President.

2064

Current ToyCo logo with "Flower" as mascot introduced.

2068

Shenji Watanabe dies of a heart attack.

2071

ToyCo sponsored investigators form the main contingent of the UN investigation into SciCon and Brute Force involvement in the missile launch at the planetoid Visitor.

Brute Force field advisors face off against UN Rovers in the demilitarized zone set up after the Teutonic Separation. Peace negotiations resume after two weeks of intense fighting.

2072

ToyCo sponsored investigators conduct an independent probe into the Zgrav arena tragedy that killed eXtreme co-president Emil Perez and eight other athletes.

2073

ToyCo contributes funds and Rover materials to assist in the joint GEC exploration of Visitor.
