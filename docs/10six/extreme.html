eXtreme History

 

2024

HiTek Sports founded in Portland, Oregon by Richard Perez. The four-person workshop makes custom planks for snow, street, and surf.

2026

Lana Thompson wins World Snowboard Championship on a HiTek board.

The company expands its product line to include leather sporting goods, making custom shoes and boots as well as footballs, soccer balls, and volleyballs.

2027

HiTek football chosen as the official ball for the World Cup. Major distribution contract signed, giving access to global markets.

2029

HiTek takes over its own distribution without any loss of retail presence. Three additional manufacturing plants open in Boston, Tokyo, and Bonn.

2030

HiTek opens its first brand name retail outlets in its manufacturing cities. Plans drawn up for 10 additional stores in North America, Europe, and Japan.

2038

HiTek Intensive Training School (HITS) opened in Portland, a Kindergarten through High School athletics-rich private school. Perez’s twin children, Emil and Anna are enrolled.

2046

National Private Schools Association awards HITS its Innovation in Education award.

2048

HiTek, in partnership with major U. S. sports associations, founds the World Sporting League. The WSL charter is to encourage international team competition in globally popular sports, extending the goals and challenges to national teams.

HiTek receives exclusive equipment rights and preferred advertising relationships with the WSL and its stadiums.

2050

Emil and Anna successfully lobby the WSL to include a series of competitions in "extreme" sports categories.

2052

Richard Perez dies. Anna and Emil are appointed as joint chief executives of the company.

2053

HiTek launches its Sport Development Program to make use of new technologies in sports contexts.

2054

The Sport Development Program introduces Polarity Surfing, Stratosphere Diving, and Virtual Termina.

2056

Polarity Surfing and Stratosphere Freestyle Diving become officially sponsored by WSL. Emil and Anna are top competitors in both.

2060

Company image restructured to reflect the character of its leadership. The current "eXtreme" name and logo are approved.

Partnerships announced with several VidNet broadcast services, as well as worldwide music publishers. The media gets familiar with eXtreme.

2063

eXtreme reaches the $100 billion yearly earnings mark.

The United Nations Global Economic Council selects eXtreme to be a member of its Economic Advisory Committee.

2065

Joint venture with Brute Force to create Virtual Combat, a battle sporting game that employs cybernetic jerseys and a virtual playfield.

2066

The World Sporting League turns down the petitions to have Virtual Combat and Virtual Termina introduced as exhibition competitions despite the Perez’ personal backing. eXtreme terminates the Brute Force consulting contract and pulls Virtual Combat in house. All support of and investment in the WSL is pulled, and eXtreme announces its own international competition series, Adrenaline, consisting entirely of unique extreme sports.

2067

Brute Force loses its suit for partial interest and profit of Virtual Combat. eXtreme cancels advertising contracts and sells all of its stock in Brute Force and its subsidiaries.

Massive electronic assault on project datastores repulsed. eXtreme contracts several independent investigators, but no culprit determined.

2068

Sport Development Program announces Zgrav, a zero gravity team sport akin to rugby, played with magnetic grappling lines inside of a large, open zero G chamber. Partnership with SciCon to design and produce prototype orbiting Zgrav arenas.

2070

eXtreme’s bid to join the Global Economic Council narrowly defeated. Deciding vote was cast by GEC member Brute Force.

2071

SciCon removed from the GEC for launching private payloads with destructive potential at the planetoid Visitor.

eXtreme accepts the invitation to fill the open slot on the council.

2072

Zgrav becomes part of the Adrenaline competition.

Explosion on Zgrav station Olympia causes a hull breach that kills 9 Zgrav players, including eXtreme co-president Emil Perez. Anna Perez narrowly escapes the same fate by luckily snagging her tether on a goal support. UN special investigators cannot find conclusive evidence to determine whether the explosion resulted from a malfunctioning stabilizing thruster or whether the device was sabotaged.

Anna Perez announces that her brother’s spirit will always remain within the company.

2073

eXtreme agrees to fund and participate in the exploration of Visitor.
