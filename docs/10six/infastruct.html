InfraStruct History

 

2021

Carl Meine establishes a small company in Hannover, Germany called Neuhaus, which specializes in prefabricated construction components, siding, and polymer materials.

2024

Neuhaus structures used extensively to provide temporary shelters for the citizens of Konstanz in southern Germany after unusually heavy rains caused the flood of the Bodensee. Neuhaus also wins several contracts to repair and replace many of the ruined buildings and homes throughout the city.

Moved by the flood experience, Meine begins designing fluoropolymer fabrics in the hopes that they could assist in flood prevention.

2025

Severe El Nino related flooding staved off in Obregon, Mexico. Using Meine’s experimental polymer sheeting, the Yagui River was held to its banks past the city, the airport, and the commercial Port of Guaymas. Total materials cost of $30,000 for the sheeting estimated to have prevented at least $200 million in damage.

Fluoropolymer sheeting patent filed under the name PolyStruct.

2026

Emergency service organizations around the world commission customized PolyStruct materials for areas troubled by floods and mudslides.

2027

New PolyStruct plants built in Mexico City and Taiwan. PolyStruct finds other applications in commercial structures and as translucent, waterproof, UV-filtering tenting for large open areas, such as stadiums, malls, and open-air plazas.

Neuhaus launches a Development and Future Products division under the name InfraStruct.

2028

Conventional building materials researchers added to the InfraStruct Development group.

2029

InfraStruct division announces a transparent form of PolyStruct with the strength of aluminum but ten times the flexibility.

Neuhaus creates a business development plan to create sun-safe outdoor areas --- parks, beaches, and sporting areas under tents of transparent PolyStruct to keep out rain and UV radiation.

2032

Neuhaus officially changes its corporate identity to InfraStruct and adopts the new logo.

2036

Partner research with OneWorld leads to the creation of a passive polymer air filter that is low cost enough to be used at residential pollution points such as chimneys and stove vents. Estimated reduction of up to 10% of carbon dioxide, carbon monoxide, and particulate carbons if employed across an industrialized area.

PolyStruct medical applications announced, which include room filtration sheets for asthmatics, allergy sufferers, and patients with other respiratory ailments.

2039

InfraStruct becomes a charter member of the United Nations new Global Economic Council.

2045

Commercial release of InfraSeal, a bitumen fluoropolymer developed to prevent erosion and deterioration on historic structures. The transparent sealant acts as a breathable but fire resistant, water resistant, UV reflective shell that improves durability and tensile strength of simple materials such as stone and wood.

2052

Carl Meine appoints Ulrich Spieler to replace him as President and CEO of InfraStruct.

2055

InfraStruct develops inexpensive metal infused polymers as barricades for United Nations peacekeeping forces. These barricade walls are deployed in the border conflict between Ethiopia and Sudan and were used to create defendable corridors that would also shield refugee migrations. Because they could be cheaply and quickly replaced, the barricades were simply too costly to be repeatedly assaulted.

2057

InfraStruct creates a line of interlocking prefabricated fortification components for UN peacekeepers and Red Cross units. These fortified areas could be quickly constructed to provide protection around a base camp, staging area, or mobile hospital.

2059

Despite criticism from the United Nations General Assembly, InfraStruct makes its fortification components commercially available.

2065

InfraStruct forms a partnership with Huber Arms to integrate stationary defensive weapon systems into turret structures that can be integrated into its basic fortification components.

Carl Meine sells all of his shares of InfraStruct stock and resigns his position on the Board of Directors.

2070

InfraStruct launches two flyby satellites to collect independent data on the planetoid Visitor.

2073

InfraStruct agrees to help fund and provide construction materials for the structures that will need to be built during the exploration of Visitor.
